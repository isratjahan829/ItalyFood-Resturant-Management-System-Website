About Maintenance

1. Regular Equipment Cleaning and Maintenance:

Performing daily, weekly, and monthly cleaning tasks on kitchen equipment, refrigeration units, and dining area elements ensures the longevity and proper functioning of these assets. This helps in preventing malfunctions and maintaining a sanitary environment.

2. Assigned Responsibilities:

Clearly defining responsibilities for different staff members ensures that tasks are consistently and effectively completed. Back-of-house staff may handle daily kitchen maintenance, while facilities maintenance staff take care of monthly and quarterly tasks. This division of responsibilities helps maintain a systematic approach to preventive maintenance.

3. Systematic Inspection of Building and Equipment:

Regular inspections of the building exterior, HVAC systems, refrigeration units, and kitchen equipment contribute to identifying issues early on. This proactive approach helps prevent major breakdowns, reduces safety risks, and enhances overall efficiency in the restaurant operations.

Back of the House

Daily maintenance tasks in a restaurant include cleaning and maintaining various equipment, such as fryers, ovens, and refrigerators. Front-of-the-house tasks involve cleaning furniture and POS equipment, while building systems and exteriors require regular checks for safety and functionality. Additionally, weekly maintenance tasks ensure ongoing preventative measures for a well-operated restaurant.
